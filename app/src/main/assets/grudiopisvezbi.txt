Lie back on a flat bench. Using a medium width grip lift the bar from the rack and hold it straight over you with your arms locked. That is the starting position. From that position, breathe in and begin coming down slowly until the bar touches your middle chest, hold it for a few seconds and push the bar back to the starting position as you breathe out. Focus on pushing the bar using your chest muscles.
Lie bac on a incline bench. Using a medium-width grip lift the bar from the rack and hold it straight over you with your arms locked. This will be your starting position. As you breathe in, come down slowly until you feel the bar on your upper chest. Hold it for a few seconds and then bring the bar back to the starting position as you breathe out and push the bar using your chest muscles.
Sit on the machine with your back flat on the pad. Adjust the machine so that your upper arms are positioned parallel to the flor and take hold of the handles. This will be your starting position. Push the handles together slowly as you squeeze your chest in the middle and breathe out while holding it for a few seconds. Return back to the starting position slowly as you inhale until your chest muscles are fully streched.
Hold the pulleys in each hand, place them on a high position and step forward with one leg while pulling your arms together in front of you. This will be your starting position. While breathing in, with a slight bend on your elbows, extend your arms to the side in a wide arc until you feel a strech on your chest. Then return your arms back to the starting position as you breathe out. 
Secure your legs at the end of the decline bench, lie down with dumbbell in each hand and move them in fron of you at shoulder width and rotate your wrists forward so that your palms are facing away from you. This will be your starting position. As you breathe out push the dumbbells up using your pectoral muscles, and hold for a few seconds. Then bring your arms down to the starting position as you breathe in.
Stand up with your torso upright while holding a barbell at a shoulder-width grip. Your palms should be facing forward and the elbows should be close to the torso. This will be your starting position. Start lifting the weight as you breathe out until the bar is at shoulder level. Then slowly start returning the bar at starting position while you breathe in.
Lie against an incline bench, with your arms holding a barbell and hanging down in a horizontal line. This will be your starting position. While keeping the upper arms stationary and breathing out, curl the weight up as high as you can while squeezing the biceps. After that slowly go to the starting position as you inhale.
Hold a barbell with both hands, palms up and a few inches apart. Stand with your torso straight and head up. This will be your starting position. Curl the bar up in a semicircular motion until the forearms touch your biceps. Exhale while you perform this motion, and then inhale while you slowly go back to the starting position.
Sit down on a flat bench holding one dumbbell in front of you between your legs. Rotate your palm until it is facing forward away from your thigh. This will be your starting position. Curl the weights forward while contracting the biceps as you breathe out. After that slowly go back to the starting position.
Sit down on a Preacher Curl Machine and select the weight. Place your hands on the machine handles with your palms facing up. This will be your starting position. Then slowly pull the handles while you exhale. Then slowly go back to the starting position as you inhale.
